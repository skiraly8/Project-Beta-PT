About Me

I am a third-year Doctor of Physical Therapy (DPT) student at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas with a strong interest in both orthopedic and sports rehabilitation, particularly with a focus on climbing injuries. I have a passion to not only maximize the potential of my patients, but to also be a top-tier clinician in the field.

Beyond my clinical rotations, I am a collaborator with The Climbing Doctor, where I help produce rehab and prehab content for the @theclimbingdoctor Instagram community. My work includes a featured article on hypermobility for the Climbing Doctor website and a guest appearance on his podcast, discussing specialized strategies for climbers.

Based in Seattle, I focus on empowering athletes through specific loading protocols and movement education to ensure they don't just recover, but improve.

Outside of physical therapy, I am an avid trail runner, climber, soccer player, cyclist, snowboarder, traveler, backpacker, and enjoy all things outdoors.

FAQ

Standard physical therapy often lacks the sport-specific knowledge required for high-level athletes. At Project Beta, we speak the language of the "project." Whether you are navigating a pulley strain on a boulder or shin splints on a trail, we use evidence-based loading protocols designed specifically for the demands of your sport.

Project Beta is an out-of-network provider and does not accept insurance. We welcome clients to pay with: Credit, debit, Venmo, and Zelle.

Clients will be offered an itemized receipt of their session (a superbill) that they may submit to their insurance company for reimbursement if they have out-of-network benefits. A superbill is a detailed billing statement that includes ICD-10 codes, CPT codes, total charges, details about the provider, and more.

Wear what you do your sport in! For climbers, athletic clothing that allows full range of motion is best. If we are meeting in-person, feel free to bring your climbing shoes.

We start with a deep dive into your injury history and specific athletic goals. From there, we perform a comprehensive movement assessment and sport-specific strength testing. By the end of the hour, you’ll have a clear understanding of the "crux" of your issue and a personalized roadmap to get back to full performance.

Follow-up sessions focus on the "beta" of your recovery. We refine your loading protocols, assess progress through performance metrics, and adjust your plan as you return to higher-intensity activities. These are active sessions designed to ensure long-term resilience, not just a quick fix.
